## üëã About Me

I am a soft matter physicist at [Durham University](https://durham.ac.uk), where I lead the [**Computational Multiphase Physics (CoMPhy) Lab**](https://comphy-lab.org). 

Our [research](https://comphy-lab.org/research) focuses on **non-Newtonian free-surface flows** and **soft matter singularities**‚Äîfluid phenomena (like drops, bubbles, jets, and sheets) where interfaces undergo rapid, dramatic changes. Using high-fidelity simulations, theoretical modeling, and collaborative experiments, we investigate events such as:
- üíß Droplet impacts and coalescence
- ü´ß Bubble bursting dynamics
- üåä Jet formation and breakup
- üìÑ Liquid sheet fragmentation

We use complex (non-Newtonian) fluids as model systems to explore how microstructural stresses influence continuum-scale dynamics, connecting fundamental insights to real-world applications across industry and nature.
